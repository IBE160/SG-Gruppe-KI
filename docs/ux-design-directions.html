Core Design Principles

Dark First, Energetic Accents

Employ a dark, modern theme as the default to create a focused, immersive experience. Use the primary vibrant green to draw attention to key actions and successes.

Conversational & Guided

Utilize a conversational UI (chat bubbles, AI avatar) for onboarding and feedback to make the experience feel personal and supportive. Guide users with clear progress indicators.

Clear Visual Hierarchy

Use typography, spacing, and color to create a clear hierarchy. Large, bold headlines for screens, and clear separation for interactive components.

Sticky Actions & Navigation

Keep primary actions and navigation persistent at the bottom of the screen for easy, one-handed access, especially in key flows like onboarding and workouts.

Interactive & Tappable

Present choices as large, rounded, tappable buttons or chips. Use clear visual states (selected, unselected, disabled) to provide immediate feedback.

Celebrate & Reinforce

Use positive reinforcement through AI messages, celebratory screens ("Workout Complete!"), and highlighting achievements ("New PR!") to keep users motivated.

Key UI Patterns

AI Conversational Bubbles

Used for onboarding, AI feedback, and contextual messages. Typically includes an AI avatar, a label, and a speech bubble with a distinct background color.

Full-Width, Rounded Buttons

The primary call-to-action is a full-width, rounded button with a background of `primary` green and dark text, ensuring it is the most prominent element.

Segmented Controls

Used for mutually exclusive choices like 'kg/lbs' or mood selection. A container holds options, with the selected item having a distinct background to indicate its state.

Fixed Bottom Navigation

A consistent, semi-transparent navigation bar is fixed to the bottom, providing access to the app's main sections: Dashboard, Workout, History, and Settings.
